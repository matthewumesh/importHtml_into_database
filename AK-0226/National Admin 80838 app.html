Parents and Friends,

As many of you may have heard, the Brooks family lost their son, Joshua, on Tuesday. Josh was at the Trail Life meeting on Monday and appeared to be in perfect health. He went to school on Tuesday but reported to the school nurse with a headache. Very shortly thereafter, he lost consciousness, was rushed to the hospital and was diagnosed with bleeding in the brain. Emergency surgery immediately ensued and, despite heroic efforts by the team at the Providence Medical Center Pediatric Unit, Josh passed away in the early afternoon. We can't say enough about the love and professionalism shown by the practitioners at Providence - they are amazing. In his short lifetime, Josh was blessed and loved and this has been a shock to us all. However, we can rest in the knowledge that God is good and that as a believer Josh is with Christ in heaven today.

Services for Joshua will be held at Community Covenant on Saturday, Sept. 17th at 10 am. We invite all who can to come support the Brooks in this difficult time. Please feel free to pass on this invitation to any who may be interested. Trail Life boys and AHG girls who wish to come in uniform are encouraged to do so. Josh is to be buried wearing his TL uniform as he was very proud to have been a long-time member. Military service members may wear the appropriate service dress uniform or the Trail Life uniform, based on your preference. Following the service, Josh will be laid to rest at the Angelus Memorial Park in Palmer.

The Brooks have been extremely giving, generous, supportive and loving to the AHG and Trail Life Families and the local community and are a wonderful example of Christ's love given to others. Many of you have asked how you can help. There have been a couple of memorial funds set up for the Brooks family. In lieu of flowers, please consider donating to either of the two funds. Your gifts will be appreciated more than you can imagine to help offset the significant hospital bills and costs associated with the funeral. I pray that we can all come together in the body of Christ to serve Danny, Joyce, Elizabeth, Clay and Alex in their time of need.
